<p><p>Magnetostrictive sensors utilize the principle of magnetostriction to detect position and movement, falling into two main types: analog and digital. Analog magnetostrictive sensors provide continuous output signals, reflecting precise position measurements, making them ideal for applications requiring fine resolution. In contrast, digital types output discrete signals, typically suitable for simpler applications with lower resolution demands. Both types serve various industries, including automation, robotics, and aerospace, offering reliable performance based on specific measurement needs and system integrations.</p></p>
