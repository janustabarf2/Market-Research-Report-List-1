<p><p>The reference voltage source market is segmented into series and parallel voltage sources. Series voltage sources provide a stable output voltage by connecting multiple power sources in a series configuration, ensuring consistent voltage levels with minimal ripple. In contrast, parallel voltage sources combine multiple sources to achieve improved current capacity while maintaining a uniform voltage output. Both types serve critical applications in precision electronics, testing equipment, and instrumentation, driving demand for reliable reference voltages in various industrial and consumer sectors.</p></p>
