<p><p>The handheld refractometers market is segmented into two main types: digital and analog (simulation). Digital refractometers offer enhanced accuracy, easy reading on LCD displays, and often include built-in temperature compensation, making them user-friendly and efficient. In contrast, analog refractometers rely on a simple optical design with a scale for manual reading, appealing to traditional users seeking straightforward functionality. Both types serve various industries, including food and beverage, agriculture, and pharmaceuticals, ensuring precise measurement of liquid refractive index.</p></p>
