<p><p>Transmucosal drug delivery devices offer efficient routes for medication absorption through the mucous membranes. The buccal route enables drugs to be placed between the gums and cheek, allowing for rapid absorption directly into the bloodstream. The rectal route involves administering medications through the anus, benefiting patients who may have difficulty swallowing. The nasal route utilizes the nasal cavity for quick absorption due to its extensive vascularization, providing an alternative for systemic delivery, particularly for drugs that are poorly absorbed orally.</p></p>
