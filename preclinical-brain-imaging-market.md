<p><p>The preclinical brain imaging market encompasses various modalities, each serving distinct research needs. Computed Tomography (CT) provides detailed structural images using X-rays; Positron Emission Tomography (PET) offers insights into metabolic processes through radiotracers. Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI) delivers high-resolution soft tissue images without radiation. Single Photon Emission Computed Tomography (SPECT) evaluates blood flow and activity levels. Other imaging techniques may include hybrid systems and fluorescent imaging, expanding capabilities for studying brain functions and disorders in animal models.</p></p>
