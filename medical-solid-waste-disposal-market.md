<p><p>The medical solid waste disposal market comprises several key methods. Incineration involves burning waste at high temperatures, reducing it to ash and gases, typically used for hazardous materials. Landfills are designated sites for burying waste, often utilized for non-hazardous types. Autoclaving uses steam and pressure to sterilize and reduce the volume of waste, rendering it safe for disposal. Chemical treatment involves using chemical agents to neutralize hazardous components. Other methods may include recycling or composting, depending on the waste type.</p></p>
