<p><p>Ligases are essential enzymes in molecular biology, used to join DNA fragments. The E. coli DNA ligase is often utilized in cloning and genetic engineering due to its efficiency in sealing nicks in double-stranded DNA. T4 DNA ligase, derived from bacteriophage T4, excels in linking cohesive ends and is widely used in constructing recombinant DNA. Mammalian ligases offer high fidelity for eukaryotic applications, while thermostable ligases, sourced from extremophiles, provide stability during high-temperature reactions in various biotechnological processes.</p></p>
